The spider's perspective is a pixelated image. Spiders have no compound eyes, only one eye, no more than 12, generally 8, and even some cave dwelling spiders do not have monocular eyes. Monocular is a relatively simple photoreceptor which can only sense the intensity of light, but can not see the image.



最新的研究发现，在捕捉猎物时，跳蛛的侧眼首先感知到 运动中目标的眼睛，然后依靠前中眼对目标物瞄准。跳蛛的 8 只眼睛中，前中眼的视网膜很特别， 具有 4 个感光细胞层。不同波长的光线经过前中眼的晶状体后会在 4 个不同的感光细胞层上形成 可测量的色差。在跳蛛视网膜的最里层和倒数第二层感光细胞层中的光感受器，含有感受绿光的 视色素，但是绿光只是聚焦在最里层。这就意味着视网膜的倒数第二层总是会接收到散焦( 模糊) 的图像。后续的行为学实验证明，跳蛛正是通过对比这两层的图像，并根据模糊图像的散焦程度推 算出与猎物之间的真实距离。

The latest research found that when catching prey, the side eyes of jumping spiders first perceive the eyes of the moving target, and then aim at the target with the front and middle eyes. Among the 8 eyes of the jumping spider, the retina of the anterior and middle eyes is very special, with four photoreceptor layers. Different wavelengths of light pass through the lens of the anterior and middle eyes and form measurable color differences on four different photoreceptor cell layers. The photoreceptors in the innermost and penultimate photoreceptor layers of the retina of the jumping spider contain the pigment that senses green light, but the green light is only focused on the innermost layer. This means that the penultimate layer of the retina always receives defocused (blurred) images. The following behavioral experiments show that the jumping spider can calculate the real distance from its prey by comparing the images of the two layers and according to the defocus degree of the blurred image.
